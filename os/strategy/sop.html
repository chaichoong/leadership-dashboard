🎯 1. Overview & Purpose

💡 What It Does

The Objective & Strategy OS gives each business a single living document covering its 9-year vision, 3-year target, 1-year target, quarterly priority projects, and monthly stepping stones. An AI wizard (“Boardroom Mentor”) interviews the founder section-by-section, pushes back on vague answers, and writes the finished plan straight into Airtable.

Each record is stored per Business × Quarter × Year. The plan is reviewed and iterated every quarter; prior quarters are preserved for reflection.

💬
Mental model: Objective = why we exist (annual). Strategy = how we win this quarter. Quarterly Projects = the three things that, if done, make the quarter a success.

🚪 2. How to Access

  1. Open the Operations Director dashboard and sign in with your Airtable Personal Access Token (PAT).
  2. In the left sidebar, click 🎯 Objective & Strategy OS (second entry, under Leadership Dashboard OS).
  3. Pick a Business, Quarter, and Year. If a record exists for that combination it loads; otherwise you see an empty-state prompt to start the AI Wizard.
  4. If you land here from a fresh session, the PAT is remembered via localStorage — no second sign-in needed.
🔗
Standalone URL: os/strategy/index.html. Loaded in-app via iframe from the main shell.

🧙 4. The AI Wizard — Boardroom Mentor Voice

🎭 Persona

The wizard speaks as Boardroom Mentor — a composite virtual board (Chair, CFO, COO, CMO, Head of People, Real Estate Strategist, Philanthropy Steward). Direct, analytical, UK English, no motivational fluff. The full prompt lives at js/prompts/boardroom-mentor.js and is shared across every AI feature on the platform, not just this OS.

📌
Why it matters: the rules of the critique (specific · testable · sized for the horizon) come from that single prompt file. Update the voice in one place, every wizard improves.
🛠️ How It Works, Step by Step
  1. Founder clicks AI Wizard ▸. The panel opens on the right of the form.
  2. The wizard looks for the prior quarter’s record (Q4 previous year if current is Q1). If found, it asks a reflection question first: what hit, what missed, why?
  3. It then walks through the strategy sections in a fixed order — 9-year → 3-year target + 3 measurables → 1-year target + 3 measurables → Quarterly Project 1 + 3 stepping stones → QP2 + stones → QP3 + stones.
  4. For every answer, the wizard calls Claude Haiku with a critique prompt that returns JSON: {accept, pushback?, refined?, note?}.
  5. If the critique rejects the answer, the pushback is shown as a challenge and the founder is asked again.
  6. If accepted, the wizard may refine the founder’s wording, then writes the final text straight into the matching form field.
  7. At the end, the form is fully populated. Founder reviews, tweaks inline, hits Save changes.
✅
Acceptable answer = specific (numbers, dates, names) + testable (“done” is observable) + sized for the horizon (nine-year visionary; monthly stone = 30 days of deliverable work).
🔑 Controls
  • Send — submits your answer for critique.
  • Skip — leaves the field empty and moves to the next section. Useful for optional slots (e.g. Quarterly Project 3, third measurables).
  • × — closes the wizard. Answers already accepted are kept in the form; press Save changes to persist.

🔄 5. Quarterly Review Loop

At the start of every quarter, the founder should:

  1. Pick the new quarter in the Quarter/Year selectors.
  2. Click AI Wizard. The wizard automatically loads the previous quarter and opens with a reflection question.
  3. Work through the wizard — most of the 9-year, 3-year, and 1-year answers can be carried forward with small tweaks. The Quarterly Projects and Monthly Stepping Stones are what change.
  4. Save. The old quarter’s record stays in Airtable untouched — nothing is overwritten.
📜
Because every quarter is its own record, you can always scroll back through the history by switching the Quarter/Year dropdowns. Use this to see what you planned vs. what you shipped.

🚀 6. Projects & Tasks Hook

After saving the quarter, the wizard offers to push the three Quarterly Projects into Projects OS as proper project records, and each monthly stepping stone becomes a linked Task due at the end of its month.
