Product Architecture & Delivery Model
What each module actually IS, how it's built, how it's delivered, and how customers access it.

What Each Module Is

A self-contained HTML/JS/CSS web application — not a template, not a spreadsheet, not a SaaS login. Each module is a standalone business tool that works in a browser, stores data in the cloud via Supabase, and delivers real operational value from day one.

Built with: Claude (HTML, JavaScript, CSS)

Delivered as: Hosted web application with unique URL per customer

Data storage: Supabase free tier (50,000 rows, unlimited API calls) — cloud-persistent, no data loss

Access: Customer receives a URL. They open it. It works. No installation, no account setup, no learning curve.

CHOSEN  Delivery Model: Hosted on Free Infrastructure

Each customer's module is deployed to Netlify (free tier). Customer gets a unique URL like smithco-finance.operationsdirector.co.uk that works instantly in any browser.

Why This Works

  • Zero hosting cost — free tiers support 100+ sites
  • Instant deployment via Claude + script
  • Custom subdomain per client looks professional
  • HTTPS included automatically
  • No customer setup required — they just open a URL
  • Updates can be pushed without customer involvement
  • Scales to hundreds of customers at zero marginal cost

Watch Out For

  • Need a deployment pipeline (built in Week 3)
  • Must use Supabase for data — NOT localStorage alone
  • Semi-manual at launch; fully automate later

Cost: £0/month. Netlify free tier = 100GB bandwidth, unlimited sites. Supabase free tier = 50k rows, unlimited API calls.

Deployment time per customer: Under 5 minutes once pipeline is built.

Future Delivery Option: Bespoke Build Per Customer

Customer provides their business info, Claude generates a fully customised application, deployed to their subdomain. Justifies premium pricing (£499-999+). Add this as a premium tier once you have 10+ standard module sales and understand what customisation customers actually want. Not for launch.

Pricing Logic

These are working HTML applications, not templates. The value comparison is against monthly SaaS subscriptions. A business paying £20/user/month for ClickUp (3 users = £720/year) gets significantly more value from a one-off purchase that does the same job permanently.

Benchmark: SaaS equivalents cost £10-95/month. A one-off at 6-12 months' equivalent cost is the sweet spot — customer saves money long-term, you get cash upfront.

Ground Rules — Read This Before Day 1
These rules apply to every single day of the plan. If in doubt, come back here.

The Team

Kevin Decisions, quality control, sign-offs. 1-2 hours/day max.
Mica (Tech) Infrastructure, deployments, technical setup.
Erica May (Marketing) Content, copy, campaigns, social media.
Claude Builds everything. Code, content, copy, strategy, modules. The engine.

How this works: Claude does 90% of the actual work. Humans set Claude up, give it the information it needs, review what it produces, and handle tasks that require human access (logging into platforms, clicking buttons, making payments). Think of it as: Claude is the brain, you're the hands.

Rule 1: Claude Does the Work

Every module, every piece of copy, every code file, every marketing post, every SEO tag — Claude builds it. Humans review, give feedback, and handle things requiring a real person (logins, payments, uploads). If you're writing code or drafting long content from scratch, stop. Ask Claude.

Rule 2: Claude Asks, You Answer

Claude will ask questions to extract the information it needs. Answer clearly and completely. The better your answers, the better the output. Don't say "something useful" — say exactly what you want to see.

Rule 3: One Step at a Time

Don't skip ahead. Each day builds on the previous day's output. If you finish early, improve what's built — don't start the next phase.

Rule 4: Ship Over Perfect

Version 1 solves one core problem well. Everything else is version 2. If you're debating a feature, ask: "Does the customer need this on Day 1?" If no, it waits.

Rule 5: Daily 15-Minute Standup

Every morning, 9am. Three questions per person: What did I finish? What am I doing today? Am I blocked? That's it. No discussions. If there's a blocker, Kevin decides immediately.

Paid Ad Strategy — Claude-Managed, Iterative

Claude builds, measures, monitors, tweaks, and iterates. Start micro, scale only what works.

Phase 1: Testing (Week 1-2 post-launch) — £5-10/day = £35-70/week
Find which message + audience + platform gets clicks. 3-4 ad variations per platform. Mix of pain point hooks, demo clips, health check offers, direct purchase CTAs. UK business owners, 1-50 employees. Claude sets up, monitors daily, kills underperformers after 48 hours.

Phase 2: Optimisation (Week 3-4) — £10-20/day = £70-140/week
Double down on winners. Create lookalike audiences from visitors and health check completions. Retarget people who visited but didn't buy. A/B test landing pages.

Phase 3: Scale (Month 2+) — Only if ROI proven
Scale rule: Only increase spend when CPA is below 30% of avg module price. At £300 avg, CPA must be under £90. Target: £30-60 per sale. Scale to £50-100/day if target met.

Sales Process — Two Funnels
Direct purchase for ready buyers. Lead magnet nurture for everyone else.

Funnel A: Direct Purchase

For people who see the product, understand the value, and are ready to buy.

1
Discover: Prospect finds us via paid ad, SEO, social, or referral.
2
Explore: Views module pages, watches demo video, reads case study.
3
Purchase: Buys module via Stripe checkout on website.
4
Deploy: Module automatically deployed to their unique subdomain within minutes.
5
Onboard: In-app tutorial walks them through setup.
6
Expand: 30-day email sequence promotes additional modules and full bundle.

For people who aren't ready to buy yet but have the pain points.

1
Ad / Content: Paid ad or organic post promotes free Business Health Check.
2
Capture: Prospect completes health check, provides email.
3
Score: Health check scores their business across OPTIMISE framework, highlights weakest areas.
4
Recommend: Automated email recommends specific module based on weakest score.
5
Nurture: 5-email sequence over 2 weeks: value, pain points, social proof, limited offer.
6
Convert: Purchase module or book a call for bundle discussion.

Assumptions to Challenge

Assumption: SME owners will pay £200-500 for an HTML app they've never seen.

Challenge: Trust is the bottleneck, not price. Mitigate with: live demo, free health check, money-back guarantee (first 50), Kevin's credibility.

Assumption: Two staff + Claude can build, launch, and market in 30 days.

Challenge: Achievable with controlled scope. 30 days gives realistic breathing room. Bottleneck is module quality, not website or marketing.

Assumption: Business page organic reach is sufficient for launch traction.

Challenge: It isn't. Business pages reach 2-5% of followers. This is why paid ads are essential, not optional. Budget £150-300/month in Phase 1.

Assumption: One-off pricing beats SaaS for this market.

Challenge: One-off is easier to sell but harder to scale revenue. Consider adding optional £29-49/month "support & updates" tier after launch. Future decision, not a launch blocker.
