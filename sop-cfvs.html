🔍 1. Overview & Detection Logic

A Cash Flow Void (CFV) is created whenever expected rental income is not received by the due date. The system compares the expected monthly rent from the Tenancies table against actual bank transactions reconciled in Airtable. Any shortfall generates a CFV record automatically.

ℹ️ CFVs are generated on the 3rd of each month for the prior month's rent. This allows a 2-day grace period for bank processing delays.

📅 4. Days Overdue Calculation

The Days Overdue field is a live formula: DATETIME_DIFF(TODAY(), {Due Date}, 'days')

This value drives the colour coding on the dashboard:

0–7 days 8–30 days 31+ days
💡 The dashboard sorts CFVs by Days Overdue descending by default, bringing the most critical items to the top.

💬 5. 3-Stage Chase Sequence

1

Stage 1 — Friendly Reminder

Days 3–7

When

Triggered automatically 3 days after the due date if no payment detected.

Action

A friendly SMS and/or email is sent to the tenant reminding them of the missed payment. The tone is informal and assumes an oversight.

Template

Uses the "Friendly Reminder" template from the Communications library. Includes the amount owed and a payment link.

2

Stage 2 — Formal Notice

Days 14–21

When

If Stage 1 has not resolved the CFV within 14 days of the due date.

Action

A formal letter or email is sent referencing the tenancy agreement terms and requesting immediate payment. Copied to any guarantor on file.

Escalation

The CFV status moves to Chasing and the chase stage updates to Stage 2.

3

Stage 3 — Legal / Final Warning

Days 30+

When

If the CFV remains unpaid after 30 days and Stages 1 and 2 have been exhausted.

Action

A final warning letter is issued referencing potential legal proceedings or Section 8 notice. Kevin reviews before sending.

Decision Point

At this stage, a decision is made: negotiate a payment plan, proceed with legal action, or write off the debt. The decision is recorded in Comments.

💵 6. Payment Detection

Payments are detected through the daily bank transaction reconciliation process. When a payment is matched to a tenant with an open CFV:

  1. The reconciliation script identifies the tenant by payment reference or sender name.
  2. The payment amount is compared against the CFV amount owed.
  3. If the payment covers the full amount, the CFV status is set to Paid automatically.
  4. If a partial payment is received, the Amount Owed is reduced and a comment is added logging the partial payment.
ℹ️ Manual payment marking is also available via the dashboard for cash payments or payments received outside the normal banking flow.

🔨 7. Action Buttons

The CFV dashboard row provides the following action buttons:

Send Chase

Sends the next chase communication based on the current stage. Advances the chase stage and updates the Last Chased date.

Mark as Paid

Manually marks the CFV as paid. Prompts for a payment date and optional reference number.

Log Promise

Records a tenant's promise to pay by a specific date. Sets status to Promised and adds the promise details to Comments.

Write Off

Marks the CFV as written off. Requires a reason note. This action is irreversible and removes the CFV from active views.

📝 8. Comments System

Every CFV has a running Comments log that records all activity:

  • Auto-logged entries — Chase communications sent, status changes, payment detections, and promise recordings are all timestamped and appended automatically.
  • Manual notes — Users can add free-text notes via the dashboard detail panel. Each note is timestamped with the user's name.
  • Format — Comments use the format [YYYY-MM-DD HH:mm] [Source] Message for consistency.
💡 The Comments field serves as a full audit trail. It is used for dispute resolution and legal proceedings if escalation is required.

🔧 10. Troubleshooting

CFV not generated for a known missed payment
Check the tenant's tenancy record is active and has the correct expected rent. Verify the reconciliation script ran on the 3rd. Review the bank transactions for any mismatched references.
Chase email not sending
Verify the tenant has an email address in the Contacts table. Check the email service integration (Gmail/Make) for quota or authentication errors. Review the chase template for merge field issues.
Payment received but CFV still shows as Open
The payment may not have been reconciled yet. Run the reconciliation manually or check the transaction matching rules. The payment reference may not match the expected pattern.
Days Overdue shows negative number
This can happen if the due date is in the future. The CFV may have been created prematurely. Review the detection logic or correct the due date in Airtable.
Status dropdown not updating from the dashboard
Check that the Airtable API key has write permissions. Verify the status option IDs in config.js match the current Airtable field configuration.
