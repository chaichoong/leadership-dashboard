📋 1. Overview & Purpose

What this page does

The Inbound Comms OS is a Gmail-integrated email management tool embedded as a standalone page (follow-up.html) inside the Leadership Dashboard. It connects directly to Gmail via Google OAuth 2.0, reads emails by label, and provides a structured workflow for ensuring every inbound communication is actioned, responded to, or filed.

Key capabilities:

  • Label-based triage — view emails sorted into 9 numbered Gmail labels (plus a Property Compliance label 10)
  • AI drafting — Claude Sonnet drafts responses; Claude Haiku drafts unsubscribe requests and classifies inbox emails
  • Inbox triage — unlabelled emails are shown with AI-suggested labels; apply individually or all at once
  • Auto label management — when someone replies to a Follow Up email, the label is automatically removed
  • Airtable integration — moving an email to label 8 auto-creates a task; label 9 is set when Airtable marks that task Completed
  • Property compliance — emails on the Compliance label are parsed by AI to create certificate records in Airtable
  • Cloud sync — knowledge base and AI corrections sync between devices via Cloudflare KV
System components

🔑 2. How to Access

Signing in
  1. Open follow-up.html from the Leadership Dashboard sidebar, or navigate to it directly.
  2. The auth screen shows "Inbound Comms Tracker" with a Sign in with Google button. Click it.
  3. Approve the Google OAuth consent screen. Required scopes: gmail.modify, gmail.send, gmail.settings.basic.
  4. The app loads your Gmail labels, then automatically loads the Inbox (Unlabelled) view.
🔒
The OAuth token is stored in sessionStorage (not localStorage), so it is cleared when you close the browser tab. You will need to sign in again each session. The token is proactively refreshed 5 minutes before expiry while the page is open.
Signing out

Click the Sign out button in the top-right header. This revokes the OAuth token and returns you to the auth screen.

First-time setup: Build Knowledge Base

On first sign-in, if no knowledge base exists and you switch to the Inbox view, a prompt appears: "Build Knowledge Base?".

  1. Click Build Now (or dismiss and do it later via Settings).
  2. The app fetches the 20 most recent emails from each numbered label (1–9) and stores sender patterns and subject keywords locally.
  3. Once built, AI suggestions appear automatically whenever you load the Inbox view.
🧠
The knowledge base takes about 30 seconds to build. It is automatically synced to Cloudflare KV so other devices pick it up without rebuilding.

📤 7. Reply & Response Workflow

1

Drafting a response

Labels 1, 2, 3

Steps

  1. Click the action button (Respond, Follow up, or Delay Payment) on any email row.
  2. The Draft modal opens. A spinner shows while Claude is drafting.
  3. The original email is shown at the top for context. The From dropdown lets you select which email alias to send from (fetched from Gmail send-as settings).
  4. Review and edit the To, Subject, and Your Response fields as needed.
  5. Optionally add attachments (click Add Attachment, max 20 MB total).
  6. Optionally type feedback in Feedback / Amendments and click Revise to get a revised draft.
  7. Click the appropriate send button to send and move the email.
🤖
AI model used: Respond/Follow Up/Delay Payment use claude-sonnet-4-20250514 (max 500 tokens). Unsubscribe requests use claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 (max 300 tokens).
2

Delay payment workflow

Label 3: To Pay

The delay payment prompt is context-aware. If previous delay emails have been sent for the same thread, they are listed in the prompt so Claude can acknowledge them and avoid repeating the same wording.

After sending, the date is recorded in delay_payment_log. The "Last Update" column in the To Pay view shows how many days ago the last delay email was sent for each thread.

⚠
Clicking Send from the delay payment modal keeps the email in label 3 (To Pay). It does not move it. Move it manually via the dropdown when the payment is actually made.
3

AI self-learning from feedback

Revise feature

Every time you click Revise, the feedback is stored in response_amendments (up to 50 entries). The next time a draft is generated for any email, the most recent 20 amendments are included in the prompt so Claude adapts to your preferences over time.

Example stored amendment: Subject: "Invoice #1234", Feedback: "Make it more formal and add our company name".

4

Follow Up auto-removal

Label 2

Each time the Follow Up label is loaded, the app checks every thread. If the last message in the thread is from someone other than your Gmail send-as aliases, it concludes the recipient has replied, and automatically removes the label from all messages in the thread. A success banner shows how many threads were moved back.

🤖 8. AI Features

AI inbox label suggestions

When you load the Inbox (Unlabelled) view and a knowledge base exists, requestAISuggestions() runs automatically. Emails are sent to Claude Haiku in batches of 5 for classification.

Each suggestion has a confidence level:

  • high — green border on the suggestion badge
  • medium — amber border
  • low — red border (treat with caution)

Hover over the suggestion badge to see the AI's reason (e.g. "Invoice from known supplier").

The suggested label is pre-selected in the Apply dropdown. You can change it before clicking Apply. If you pick a different label from what the AI suggested, the correction is stored in label_corrections and synced to the cloud for future suggestions.

📊
The AI Accuracy (30 day) stat shows the percentage of suggestions that matched what you actually applied, calculated over the last 30 days. Green = 90%+, Amber = 65–89%, Red = below 65%.
AI retry logic

The AI suggestion loop retries up to 3 times on HTTP 529 (overloaded) responses, with delays of 2.5s, 5s, and 7.5s. For other API errors the batch shows "Retry later" badges.

A separate retryWithBackoff() function handles rate-limited (HTTP 429) or overloaded (HTTP 529/503) responses for general API calls, with exponential backoff starting at 1s and up to 3 retries.

AI Airtable task generation

When an email is moved to label 8 (or an inbox label of 8 is applied), generateAirtableTaskContent() calls Claude Haiku (max 300 tokens) to produce:

  • A task title starting with INBOUND: (under 100 chars)
  • An actionable description (under 500 chars)

If the AI call fails, a fallback title of INBOUND: {email subject} is used.

AI compliance extraction

When an email is in the Property Compliance label, processComplianceEmail() calls Claude Haiku (max 500 tokens) to extract:

  • cert_type: one of GSC, EICR, Landlord Insurance, Fire Alarm
  • property_name: matched against your Airtable properties list
  • renewal_date: as YYYY-MM-DD
  • status: Active or Expired

The response can be a single object or an array (for emails containing multiple certs). After creating a new cert record, older records of the same type and property are automatically marked as Expired in Airtable (batch PATCH, up to 10 at a time).

🔍 9. Filtering & Search

Local search (within the current label)

The search bar appears below the stats after emails load. Type to search with a 300ms debounce. Searches across: subject, sender name, sender email, snippet, and the full decoded body of every message in the thread.

A clear button (×) appears in the search box. Below the box, a count shows "Showing X of Y emails". Pagination resets to page 1 on each search.

Global search (across all labels)

Click the This Label scope button to switch it to All Emails. In global mode, type your query and press Enter. The app uses the Gmail API's q parameter to search (up to 100 results).

Results are displayed in a special global view showing Subject/From, Label, Date, and Age columns. Each result shows which label the email belongs to via a tag badge.

The current label's emails are saved while global search is active. Clearing the search or switching the scope back restores them.

⚠
The scope toggle button is hidden on the Inbox view (global search is always available via the Enter key in Inbox). Switching labels clears any active search.
Newsletter filter bar

When viewing label 6 (Newsletter), a filter bar appears with three buttons: All, Unread, Read. These filter the list by the local read status stored in read_newsletters. The filter combines with any active search query.

Pagination

Pages contain 50 emails each. The pagination bar shows at the bottom of the list when there is more than one page. Previous / Next buttons navigate between pages; clicking scrolls back to the top of the list.

📊 10. Airtable Task Automation

How it works

When an email is moved to label 8: Task created (via the label dropdown or the inbox Apply button), the app automatically:

  1. Checks if a task already exists for this thread (by searching the Inbound Note URL Link field for the Gmail thread ID).
  2. Calls Claude Haiku to generate a task title and description.
  3. Creates a new record in the Airtable Operations Director base with Status = Today, Priority = Urgent, Time Estimate = 15 min.
  4. Sets the Gmail thread URL as the Inbound Note URL Link so the task can be traced back.
⚠
Airtable integration requires a Personal Access Token configured in Settings. Without it, moving to label 8 will show an error and no task will be created. The email moves to the label regardless.
Auto-completion detection (label 8 → 9)

Every time you load label 8, the app queries Airtable for inbound tasks (Inbound Communication Task = true) with Status = Completed and a Gmail URL in the Inbound Note URL Link field.

Any loaded emails that match are automatically moved from label 8 to label 9: Task completed in Gmail, and removed from the current list. A success banner shows how many were moved.

🏢 11. Property Compliance Processing

Overview

Emails labelled with a label beginning with 10 (e.g. "10: Property Compliance") trigger automatic compliance certificate processing. The app uses AI to parse the email and create records in the Airtable Compliance Certs table.

Valid certificate types: GSC, EICR, Landlord Insurance, Fire Alarm.

Processing steps
  1. The app fetches all properties from the Airtable Properties table (cached in memory during the session).
  2. Claude Haiku analyses the email body and extracts cert type, property name, renewal date, and status.
  3. The property name is matched (case-insensitive) against your Airtable properties by full name or short name.
  4. A new cert record is created in the Compliance Certs table with a link back to the Gmail thread URL in the Notes field.
  5. Older Active certs of the same type and property are automatically marked Expired.
  6. The processing result is stored in compliance_processed in localStorage.
Compliance status badges
  • Processed — cert record created successfully
  • Failed or No Match — AI could not extract details or no matching property found
  • Pending — not yet processed

Failed emails can be retried via Process Now in the expanded row, or via Process All Pending in the compliance banner.

💾 12. Backup, Sync & Audit Log

Auto-backup

After every significant action (send email, apply label, build KB, etc.) the app silently saves a backup of all localStorage data to the key auto_backup_data. If no backup has been taken in the last 24 hours, one is triggered on app load.

The backup indicator in the header shows the age of the last backup (e.g. "Backup: 2h ago"). If over 24 hours old it turns red.

Manual backup & restore

Open Settings (gear icon) and scroll to Backup & Restore.

  • Export Backup: downloads a JSON file named inbound-comms-backup-YYYY-MM-DD.json containing all local data.
  • Import Backup: restores from a previously exported JSON file. If the file does not match the expected format, a confirmation prompt is shown.

Keys included in backup: label_knowledge_base, kb_last_built, ai_corrections, ai_accuracy_log, delay_payment_log, read_newsletters, unsubscribe_log, sender_name, applied_today_count, applied_today_date, response_amendments.

Cloud sync (Cloudflare KV)

On every app load (after auth), syncFromCloud() fetches the shared data from Cloudflare KV. Three keys are synced:

  • label_knowledge_base — AI label patterns
  • kb_last_built — timestamp of last KB build
  • ai_corrections — user label corrections

If the cloud version is newer (based on kb_last_built timestamp), all three keys are pulled from the cloud. If local is newer, all three are pushed to the cloud. The cloud sync indicator in the header shows: Syncing... (amber), Synced (green), or Offline (red) if the request fails.

☁
Cloud sync means the knowledge base and corrections are shared across all devices and browsers — you only need to build the KB once.
Audit log

Every action is recorded in audit_log in localStorage (up to 500 entries, newest last). Open Settings to view the last 50 entries (newest first) with timestamp, icon, and description.

Audit actions logged: label_move, email_sent, ai_draft, inbox_label, inbox_label_bulk, unsubscribe, kb_build, backup_export, backup_import, undo, cloud_sync, airtable_create, airtable_complete, compliance_cert.

From Settings you can Export Log (CSV) (downloads audit-log-YYYY-MM-DD.csv) or Clear Log (requires confirmation).

🔧 15. Troubleshooting

Page shows auth screen even though I was signed in before
The OAuth token is in sessionStorage and is cleared when the tab closes. Click "Sign in with Google" again. This is expected behaviour for security.
"Session expired. Please sign in again." error appears
The token expired mid-session (rare — the app proactively refreshes 5 minutes before expiry). Sign out and sign in again. If this happens frequently, check that the Google OAuth consent screen app is not in a restricted state.
Emails not loading — "Failed to load emails" error
Check your internet connection. If the error includes a Gmail API error message, the API may be temporarily down. Try clicking Refresh. Check the browser console for detailed error messages.
AI suggestions show "Retry later" or "Error" badges
The Claude API was overloaded (HTTP 529). The app retries up to 3 times. If still failing, click Refresh to reload the page and try again after a few minutes.
AI suggestions are inaccurate
Rebuild the knowledge base: open Settings, click "Build KB". This re-trains from the 20 most recent emails per label. Also check that past corrections are being applied — the AI accuracy % should improve over time as you correct suggestions.
"Label not found" when moving an email
The label name in the dropdown does not match a label in your Gmail account. This can happen if a label was renamed or deleted. Click Refresh to reload labels.
Airtable task not created when moving to label 8
Check Settings — your Airtable PAT must be set. The error message "Airtable token not configured" confirms this. Also check that the base ID and table ID are correct (defaults: appnqjDpqDniH3IRl / tblqB8b22hKBL4PF1).
Compliance processing shows "No Match" or "Failed"
The AI could not identify the certificate type or property. Check that the email subject/body clearly mentions one of the valid cert types (GSC, EICR, Landlord Insurance, Fire Alarm) and a property name that matches your Airtable Properties table. You can retry via the "Process Now" button in the expanded row.
"Please wait, a label change is already in progress"
A label move is rate-limited with 300ms delays per message. Wait a moment and try again. If it appears stuck, click Refresh.
Cloud sync shows "Offline" indicator
The Cloudflare Worker is unreachable. The app falls back to localStorage — all functionality still works, but knowledge base will not sync to other devices until connectivity is restored. The indicator updates automatically on the next sync attempt.
Follow Up emails not being auto-removed after replies
The auto-removal checks the last message sender against your Gmail send-as aliases. If the alias is not set up in Gmail Settings > Accounts > Send mail as, or is not verified, the comparison may fail. Ensure all your sending email addresses are listed as verified send-as aliases in Gmail.
Email body shows "(Unable to decode content)"
The email uses an unusual Base64 encoding. This is a display issue only — all actions (reply, move) still work on the email. The sender can be asked to resend in a standard format.
Attachment download fails
Attachment data is fetched on demand from the Gmail API. If the session token expired mid-download, sign in again and retry. Large attachments near the 20 MB limit may also time out.
