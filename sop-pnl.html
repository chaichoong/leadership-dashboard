Profit & Loss OS SOP v1.0

Standard Operating Procedure — Operations Director Platform

1. Purpose

The Profit & Loss (P&L) report provides a real-time view of business financial performance, derived directly from reconciled transactions in Airtable. It enables the business owner to:

  • Monitor revenue, cost of goods sold (COGS), and operating expenses by month
  • Track gross and net profit margins against a 15% net margin target
  • Identify trends, cost drivers, and opportunities via AI-powered analysis
  • Correct mis-categorised transactions without leaving the report

2. Data Source

All figures are computed from the Transactions table in the Operations Director Airtable base, filtered by:

  • Business (For Reports) — selectable between Real Estate and Operations Director
  • Date — grouped into calendar months for the selected period (1, 3, 6, or 12 months)
  • Chart of Accounts Sub-Category — classified into Revenue, COGS, or Operating Expenses using an explicit allow-list
Key field: The report uses Report Amount (not raw GBP Amount) so that multi-currency transactions are normalised.

3. Report Structure

3.1 AI Financial Analysis

Click Generate Analysis to get AI-powered insights. The AI reviews all P&L data and provides:

  • Performance summary with key metrics
  • Trend identification (revenue growth, cost creep, margin changes)
  • Areas of concern with specific figures
  • Opportunities to increase revenue or reduce costs
  • Specific actions to reach the 15% net margin target

3.3 Charts

Four visual charts update automatically with the selected business and period:

  • Revenue vs Expenses — bar chart showing revenue (green), COGS (red), OpEx (orange) by month
  • Profit Trend — line chart with gross profit, net profit, and the £10k/mo target line
  • Profit Margin Trend — line chart with GP% and NP% plus the 15% target line
  • Expense Breakdown — doughnut chart showing where money goes across all COGS + OpEx sub-categories

3.4 P&L Grid

The detailed grid shows every sub-category by month with frozen headers (scroll vertically to keep month labels visible, scroll horizontally to keep line items visible).

  • Click any value to drill down into the underlying transactions
  • Section headers: Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → OpEx → Net Profit
  • Margin rows show percentages in italic

4. Drill-Down & Editing

Clicking any non-zero value in the grid opens a transaction list showing:

  • Date and Transaction Detail
  • Report Amount (colour-coded: green positive, red negative)
  • Three editable fields (type-to-search): Category, Sub-Category, Business (For Reports)
Editing saves immediately to Airtable. You'll see a ✓ when saved successfully or ✗ if there's an error. When you close the drill-down modal, the P&L re-renders with the updated data.

5. Sub-Category Classification

The P&L uses an explicit allow-list of 27 sub-categories. Transactions whose sub-category is not in this list (e.g. Capex, Transfers, Loans, Personal) are excluded from the report.

Revenue (3 sub-categories)

Fixed Income · Variable Income · Rental Income

Cost of Goods Sold (9 sub-categories)

COGS Labour · COGS Sales Fees · COGS Product Costs · COGS Delivery Costs · COGS Commission · COGS Property Council Tax · COGS Property Utilities · COGS Property Reactive Maintenance · COGS Property Compliance

Operating Expenses (15 sub-categories)

Opex Labour · Marketing · Premises / Overheads · Insurance · Software & Subscriptions · Professional Fees · Travel & Training · Operational Supplies · Subsistence · Director Discretionary Expenses · Charity · Mortgage Interest · Loan Interest · Bank Transaction Fees · Tax

6. Best Practices

  1. Reconcile transactions first — the P&L only includes transactions with a sub-category and business link. Use the Leadership Dashboard reconciliation tab to categorise new transactions.
  2. Check the Business filter — ensure transactions are assigned to the correct business (Real Estate vs Operations Director). Use the drill-down to correct any that are wrong.
  3. Use AI Analysis regularly — generate an analysis at least monthly to catch trends early.
  4. Focus on margin, not just revenue — the 15% net margin target is more important than raw revenue growth.
  5. Compare periods — switch between 3-month and 12-month views to distinguish seasonal patterns from genuine trends.
